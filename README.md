## About me
Hi! I am an ocean biogeochemist using observations from autonomous platforms and remote sensing to investigate ocean carbon and oxygen cycling. I'm especially interested in how ocean observations can help us understand and predict ocean changes and inform human action. I'm currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the Canadian-Pacific Robotic Ocean Observing Facility [C-PROOF](https://cproof.uvic.ca/) and the Pacific Rim Ocean Data Mobilization and Technology [PRODIGY](https://prodigy.eoas.ubc.ca/) programs. 

## Active projects
- **Oxygen dynamics on the shelf** — Utlizing data from ocean gliders to investigate local drivers of oxygen variabilty in Queen Charlotte Sound, British Columbia. 
  
- **Global controls on annual carbon export** - The amount of carbon annually removed from contact with the atmosphere by biota, known as annual net community production or ANCP, is difficult to characterize, but essential to understanding the ocean's role in the global carbon cycle. We use biogeochemical Argo float data to estimate ANCP, depth-resolved respiration rates, and the amount of carbon available to be exported in regions with large seasonal phytoplankton blooms. We find that where mean euphotic depth becomes shallower than the maximum mixed layer depth, deep winter mixing re-entrains a variable but significant ($\sim$20-50\%) of seasonally exported carbon. This process leads to similar ANCP across polar, subpolar, and subtropical ocean regions. Future changes in stratification may influence the relationship between seasonal and annual carbon export. (Paper coming soon!)

- **Phytopankton bloom phenology** - Seasonal changes in phytoplankton biomass evolve due to changes in mixing, light, nutrients, and grazing pressure. In the Southern Ocean and southern subtropical oceans, long term trends in satellite ocean color, phytoplankton bloom phenology estimated from satellite chlorophyll, and net primary production (NPP), suggest climate driven chagnes in surface ocean ecosystems. We compare seasonal changes in surface ocean chlorophyll and particulate backscatter to upper ocean integrated chlorophyll and particulate backscatter to provide insight into what signals at the surface do or do not reveal about changes in upper ocean phytoplankton biomass.
